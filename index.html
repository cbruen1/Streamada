G.A.D.A.

Grange Armada Development Centre

Our mission is to establish Grange as the Armada Centre for Ireland through: the protection and conservation of the Armada Wreck site at Streedagh; the development of an Armada Interpretive Centre at the Courthouse in Grange; an annual series of events commemorating the Armada story.

Streedagh Strand

The Streedagh Wrecks

It was here at Streedagh, that three ships of the Levant squadron La Lavia (728 tons) vice flagship of the squadron, La Juliana (860 tons) and Santa Maria de Visón (666 tons) foundered on September 25th 1588.

Staad Abbey

Monastery of monks

On returning from the beach, if the visitor walks to the top of the lane directly opposite the Spanish Armada Memorial, as you look across the intervening farmland, it is possible to discern close to the shoreline, the single surviving gable wall of Staad Abbey. To where, on gaining the shore do Cúellar walked in search of help, unfortunately the English had been there before him.

About

Ever since the tragic loss of three great ships of the Spanish Armada and over 1,100 men; soldiers and sailors, noblemen and commoners; on the beach at Streedagh on that fateful day, 21st September 1588, the people of North Sligo have commemorated in folk memory the plight of all those poor lost fellow souls.
